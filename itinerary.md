## TUESDAY – MARCH 31, 2026

### 🇯🇵 Tokyo (Day 1) — Cultural Tokyo: Asakusa → Ueno → Sumo Festival

**Morning**

* **8:00 AM** — **Senso-ji Temple** ⭐
  * 🚶 500m walk from apartment (~7 min). Go early — Nakamise shopping street opens at 9–10 but temple grounds open from 6 AM
  * Main hall: 6:00–17:00. Draw an *omikuji* fortune (¥100). The thunder gate (Kaminarimon) is the iconic photo spot
* **9:30 AM** — **Nakamise-dori shopping** (¥500–1,000)
  * Same area as Senso-ji. Snacks, souvenirs, traditional crafts
  * Try *ningyo-yaki* (filled cakes) and *age-manju* (fried dumplings)
* **10:30 AM** — Walk or subway to **Ueno Park**
  * 🚇 2 km — Walk 20 min along Kappabashi-dori (interesting kitchenware street!) or Metro Ginza Line Asakusa → Ueno (1 stop, 3 min, ¥170)
  * **Cherry blossoms will be at peak!** 800+ cherry trees. Walk along Shinobazu Pond. Free

**Late Morning / Afternoon**

* **11:30 AM** — **Tokyo National Museum** ⭐
  * 🚶 Inside Ueno Park, 10 min walk from park entrance
  * ¥1,000/adult (~$7), free under 17. Open 9:30–17:00 (Tue–Sun)
  * Japan's oldest and largest museum. Samurai swords, armor, ancient ceramics — the 10yo history buff will be in heaven. Allow 1.5–2 hours
* **1:00 PM** — **Ameyoko Market** lunch (¥500–1,000/person)
  * 🚶 Just south of Ueno Park along JR tracks — 5 min walk from museum
  * Bustling outdoor market under the train tracks. Street food: *takoyaki*, grilled seafood, fresh fruit
* **2:30 PM** — **Ueno Zoo** (optional) — ¥600/adult (~$4), free under 12 (10yo FREE)
  * Inside Ueno Park, near the museum. Giant pandas, gorillas. Good if kids have energy. Skip if museum was enough
* **4:00 PM** — Walk along the Sumida River back to Asakusa for more cherry blossom views

**Evening — Tokyo Sumo Festival ✅ BOOKED**

* **4:30 PM** — Return to Asakusa, freshen up
* **5:00 PM** — Head to Ginza (Asakusa Line → Higashi-Ginza, ~30 min)
* **5:30 PM** — Arrive Ginza Inz 1, B1 (directly connected to Ginza Station)
* **6:00 PM** — Tokyo Sumo Festival show begins
  * Watch live sumo matches, learn basic sumo movements, challenge a wrestler
  * Welcome drink included with ticket
  * **Order food on-site** (meals not included with ticket — additional charge)
  * Cost: ¥48,000–57,600 tickets (~$320–385) + ¥5,000–10,000 food (~$33–67). Regular seat: ¥9,600/adult, child (4–12) ¥8,000. 13yo pays adult price, 10yo gets child price
* **~8:30 PM** — Show ends, train back to Asakusa (~30 min)
* **Night** — Optional **Sumida River walk** — if energy permits, walk to Azumabashi Bridge for a stunning nighttime view of Tokyo Skytree lit up across the river

**Day spend estimate:** ~$500–610 for the group (Sumo Festival is the big spend of the trip!)

**Overnight:** Tokyo (Sunbright Asakusa 101)

## WEDNESDAY – APRIL 1, 2026

### 🇯🇵 Tokyo (Day 2) — Modern Tokyo: Akihabara → Tokyo Tower → teamLab → Kaguwa Show

**Morning**

* Breakfast near Asakusa
* **9:00 AM** — Train to **Akihabara** ⭐🎮 (¥170/person — Tsukuba Express from Asakusa, 1 stop, 5 min)
* **9:30–12:00** — Akihabara anime district — the 13yo’s paradise. Must-visit shops:
  * **Mandarake** (12:00–20:00) — Pre-owned anime goods, rare figurines, vintage manga. 8 floors. Budget ¥2,000–5,000
  * **Radio Kaikan** (10:00–20:00) — 30+ specialty stores: K-Books (manga), Kaiyodo (figurines), Volks (models)
  * **Super Potato** (11:00–20:00) — Retro video game heaven. Playable retro arcade on top floor — both kids will love this (¥100–300 per play)
  * **Gachapon machines** everywhere — ¥200–500 each, addictive capsule toy collectibles
* **12:00 PM** — Lunch in Akihabara (¥3,000–4,000 for group)

**Afternoon**

* **1:30 PM** — Train to **Tokyo Tower** ⭐🗼 (¥250/person — JR Keihin-Tohoku Line from Akihabara to Hamamatsucho, 5 stops, 12 min then 15 min walk)
* **2:00–3:30 PM** — **Tokyo Tower** — Main Deck: ¥1,500/person (~$10). Group cost: ¥7,500 (~$50)
  * Hours: 9:00–23:00. The 10yo specifically wants this! 333m tall. Glass floor panels at 150m
  * Both Decks option: ¥3,500/person (top deck at 250m — worth it on clear days, book online for ¥3,300)
  * Clear spring weather = possible Mt. Fuji views
* **3:30 PM** — Train to **teamLab Planets** ⭐✨ (¥400/person — Oedo Line from Akabanebashi to Shiodome, transfer to Yurikamome Line to Shin-Toyosu, ~25 min)
* **4:00–6:15 PM** — **teamLab Planets** — Adults: ¥4,200+ (~$28), 13yo: ¥2,800 (~$19), 10yo: ¥1,500 (~$10). Group total: ~¥17,100 (~$114)
  * Walk barefoot through water (knee-deep in places), wading through immersive light installations
  * Athletics Forest: climbing, bouncing spheres, slides. "Multi Jumping Universe" is ages 20 and under only (kids-only!)
  * Bring shorts or use free rental shorts. Free lockers. Allow 2–3 hours
  * ⚠️ **Leave by 6:15 PM** to make Kaguwa show. Quick bite option: Vegan Ramen UZU inside teamLab (~¥1,100/bowl)

**Evening**

* **6:15 PM** — Train back to Asakusa (~25 min — Yurikamome from Shin-Toyosu → Oedo Line → Asakusa)
* ✅ **7:00 PM — Asakusa Kaguwa** (doors open) — 1-12-10 Asakusa, Taito-ku. Enter from covered shopping arcade side, look for lanterns, upstairs to 2F. Tickets in GetYourGuide app.
* **8:00–9:00 PM — Show** (non-verbal Japanese cabaret: oiran, samurai, traditional dance + contemporary music. 1 drink included, food available on-site. Photos with cast after.)
* 9:00 PM — Walk home (5 min). Pack for Osaka.

**Day spend estimate:** ~$250–300 for the group (teamLab is the big ticket)

**Overnight:** Tokyo (Sunbright Asakusa 101)

## THURSDAY – APRIL 2, 2026

### 🇯🇵 Tokyo → Osaka (Bullet Train Day)

**Morning – Asakusa → Shinkansen Station**

* **8:00 AM** — Breakfast at Asakusa bakery or konbini (¥1,500–2,000). Pelican Bakery is a local legend (opens 8 AM, cash only, often sells out)
* **9:00 AM** — Check out of Sunbright Asakusa 101. Final luggage check
* **9:15 AM** — Taxi to **Tokyo Station** (~¥3,000 / ~$20, ~20 min). Tell driver “Tokyo Eki, Yaesu guchi” (Yaesu exit). Don’t try to manage bags on trains
* **9:45 AM** — Arrive Tokyo Station. Follow signs for 東海道新幹線 (Tokaido Shinkansen), Platforms 14–19
* **10:00 AM** — **Buy ekiben!** 🍱 (¥1,000–1,500/person)
  * Ekiben shops are everywhere on the platform and in “Gransta” underground shopping area
  * Beloved Japanese tradition — beautifully packaged bento made for train travel. Kids will love choosing their own
  * Favorites: *torimeshi* (chicken), *makunouchi* (variety), seasonal specials

**Mid-day – Shinkansen (Bullet Train)**

* **~10:30 AM** — Board **Nozomi Shinkansen → Shin-Osaka**
* **Train:** Nozomi (fastest, most frequent)
* **Route:** Tokyo → Shin-Osaka
* **Travel time:** ~2 hr 30 min
* **Seats:** Reserved seats (sit together)
* **Cost (adult):** ~¥14,720 one-way
* **Kids (6–11):** ~50% fare
* Large luggage: reserve oversized luggage seats if needed
* 🗻 **Mt. Fuji viewing tip:** Sit on the RIGHT side of the train (seats D & E). About 40–50 min after departure, watch for Fuji between Shin-Yokohama and Shizuoka. On a clear spring day, it’s spectacular

**Arrival – Osaka**

* **~1:00 PM** — Arrive **Shin-Osaka Station**
* **1:15 PM** — Taxi to Osaka Airbnb (~¥3,000 / ~$20, ~20–25 min). Best with luggage. Subway is possible (Midosuji Line to Namba, 15 min, ¥280/person) but slow with bags

**Afternoon**

* **1:45 PM** — Check into Osaka Airbnb, unpack, rest. Explore the immediate Nihonbashi neighborhood on foot
* **3:30 PM** — **Namba Yasaka Shrine** (optional)
  * 🚶 700m from Airbnb (~10 min walk). Free
  * Giant lion-head stage (*ema*) — one of Osaka’s most photogenic spots. Quick 15-min visit, very Instagram-worthy

**Evening**

* **5:00 PM** — Walk to **Dotonbori** ⭐
  * 🚶 500m walk from Airbnb (~7 min). Osaka’s neon-lit entertainment street
  * The Glico Running Man sign is the iconic photo spot. Cross the Ebisu Bridge. Soak in the atmosphere
* **6:00 PM** — **Street food dinner** along Dotonbori (¥5,000–7,000 for group)
  * THE food street in Japan. Must-try: **takoyaki** (octopus balls — Osaka’s soul food), **okonomiyaki** (savory pancake), **kushikatsu** (deep-fried skewers), **gyoza**

**Day spend estimate:** ~$560 for the group (Shinkansen is the big cost)

## FRIDAY – APRIL 3, 2026

### 🇯🇵 Osaka (Full Day) — Castle, Blossoms & Food

**Morning**

* **8:30 AM** — **Kuromon Ichiba Market** (¥3,000–5,000 for group)
  * 🚶 800m walk from Airbnb (~10 min). “Osaka’s Kitchen” — 170+ stalls
  * Fresh sashimi, grilled seafood on sticks, tamagoyaki (sweet egg), fresh fruit. Eat breakfast here. Open from 8–9 AM
* **10:00 AM** — Subway to **Osaka Castle** ⭐🏰
  * 🚇 3.5 km — Subway Sennichimae Line from Nihonbashi to Tanimachi-Yonchome (3 stops, 6 min), then 15 min walk through castle park. ~25 min total (¥230/person)
* **10:30 AM** — **Osaka Castle** — ¥1,200/adult (~$8), kids under 15 FREE. Group cost: ¥3,600 (~$24)
  * Hours: 9:00–17:00. 8 floors of exhibits on Toyotomi Hideyoshi and the castle’s 400+ year history
  * The 10yo will love this — top floor (8F) is an observation deck with panoramic views
  * Includes the NEW Toyotomi Stone Wall Museum (opened 2025). Book e-tickets on Klook to skip the line

**Midday**

* **12:00 PM** — **Nishinomaru Garden** ⭐🌸 (¥350/person / ~$2.30)
  * Cherry blossom viewing at its finest. 300 cherry trees surrounding the castle
  * Extended hours during blossom season (March 20 – April 12): open until 9 PM with lantern illumination at night
  * This is the postcard moment of the trip — come back at dusk for illuminated blossoms
* **1:00 PM** — Lunch in **Shinsekai**
  * 🚇 Subway from Tanimachi-Yonchome to Dobutsuen-mae (Tanimachi Line, 3 stops, 6 min)
  * Retro neighborhood famous for kushikatsu (fried skewers). Daruma is the iconic chain (¥3,000–4,000 for group)
  * Tsutenkaku Tower offers another observation deck (¥900 / ~$6) if the 10yo wants more towers

**Afternoon**

* **2:30 PM** — Walk from Shinsekai to Namba area
  * 🚶 ~2 km, walk 25 min or subway Dobutsuen-mae to Namba (Midosugi Line, 1 stop, 3 min)
  * Walk through Shinsaibashi shopping street (covered arcade, perfect if it rains)
* **3:30 PM** — **Den Den Town** (Osaka’s Akihabara) 🎮
  * 🚶 500m from Airbnb (~7 min walk). It’s IN Nihonbashi — right on your doorstep
  * For the 13yo: Osaka’s anime/manga district. Smaller than Akihabara but less crowded. Mandarake has a branch here too
* **5:00 PM** — Return to Airbnb to freshen up

**Evening**

* **6:30 PM** — **Return to Nishinomaru Garden** for illuminated cherry blossoms 🌸🌙
  * 🚇 Same route as morning: subway from Nihonbashi to Tanimachi-Yonchome (~25 min total)
  * The nighttime illumination is magical — lanterns light up 300 cherry trees against the castle backdrop. Open until 9 PM during blossom season
* **OR** — Final Osaka dinner along **Dotonbori** (¥5,000–7,000). Last night — eat everything you missed yesterday
* **Night** — Pack for morning departure. Flight at 11:15 AM, need to be at KIX by ~8:30 AM
