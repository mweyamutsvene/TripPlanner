# Dinner Guide 🍽️

**Group:** 3 adults + 2 children (ages 13 & 10)
**Budget range per dinner:** ¥4,000–10,000 for the group (~$27–67) unless splurging
**Currency:** ¥150 = $1 USD | NT$32 = $1 USD

> Each evening has 3 options: **Budget**, **Mid-Range**, and **Splurge**. Pick what fits the vibe of that day. Dinner + Show combos are marked with 🎭.

## Tips for Dining with Kids in Japan & Taiwan

- **Night markets (Taiwan):** Point-and-order culture. No language barrier — just point at what looks good. Most items are ¥50–100 NT ($1.50–3 USD).
- **Vending machine ordering (Japan):** Many ramen and gyudon shops have a ticket vending machine. Insert cash, press the button with the photo, hand the ticket to the cook. Simple and fun for kids.
- **Oshibori:** You'll receive a hot/cold wet towel at most sit-down restaurants in Japan. Use it to wipe your hands (not your face — technically).
- **Water is free:** In Japan, water and tea are typically free and refilled automatically. No need to order drinks.
- **Kids' portions:** Many Japanese restaurants don't have a kids' menu but portions are naturally smaller. You can often order a regular dish to share. Rice is usually free refills or very cheap (¥100–200).
- **"Sumimasen"** (excuse me) — the magic word to get a server's attention in Japan. Raise your hand and say it clearly.
- **No tipping** in Japan or Taiwan. The bill is the bill.
- **Cash:** Carry cash in Japan (many restaurants are cash-only, especially in Asakusa). Taiwan night markets are almost entirely cash.
