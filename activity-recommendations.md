# Curated Activity Recommendations 🎯

**Group:** 3 adults + 2 children (ages 13 & 10)
**Kid interests:** 13yo — anime/manga fan | 10yo — history & landmarks (loves Tokyo Tower)
**Cherry blossoms:** Peak bloom in Tokyo Mar 26–Apr 3, Osaka Apr 2–10 — you're perfectly timed!

> These recommendations are organized by day, matching your existing itinerary. Each day is designed with a realistic pace for a family, factoring in transit time between stops.

**Home bases:**
- **Taipei:** Airbnb near Q Square / Taipei Main Station
- **Tokyo:** Sunbright Asakusa 101, 6-6-5 Asakusa, Taito City
- **Osaka:** Airbnb in Nihonbashi / Namba area

## Taipei — March 28–29

📍 Home base: **Taipei Main Station / Q Square**

Keep it light. You arrive at 5 AM after a red-eye.

## Tokyo — March 31 & April 1

📍 Home base: **Sunbright Asakusa 101** (6-6-5 Asakusa, Taito City)

You have **exactly 2 full days** in Tokyo. These need to count.

### Day 4 (Tue Mar 31) — Cultural Tokyo: Asakusa → Ueno → Cherry Blossoms

**Theme:** History, temples, cherry blossoms, and traditional Tokyo. Perfect for the 10yo.

| Time | Activity | Cost | Notes |
|------|----------|------|-------|
| **8:00 AM** | **Senso-ji Temple** ⭐ | Free | 🚶 **500m walk from apartment (~7 min).** Go early — Nakamise shopping street opens at 9–10 but the temple grounds are open from 6 AM. Main hall: 6:00–17:00. Draw an *omikuji* fortune (¥100). The thunder gate (Kaminarimon) is the iconic photo spot. |
| **9:30 AM** | Nakamise-dori shopping | ¥500–1,000 | 🚶 **Same area as Senso-ji.** Snacks, souvenirs, traditional crafts. Try *ningyo-yaki* (filled cakes) and *age-manju* (fried dumplings). |
| **10:30 AM** | Walk or subway to **Ueno Park** | ¥170 per person (subway) or ~20 min walk | 🚇 **2 km — Walk 20 min along Kappabashi-dori (interesting kitchenware street!) or Metro Ginza Line Asakusa → Ueno (1 stop, 3 min).** **Cherry blossoms will be at peak!** The park has 800+ cherry trees. Walk along Shinobazu Pond. Free. |
| **11:30 AM** | **Tokyo National Museum** ⭐ | ¥1,000/adult (~$7), free under 17 | 🚶 **Inside Ueno Park, 10 min walk from park entrance.** Japan's oldest and largest museum. Samurai swords, armor, ancient ceramics — the 10yo history buff will be in heaven. Open 9:30–17:00 (Tue–Sun). Allow 1.5–2 hours. |
| **1:00 PM** | **Ameyoko Market** lunch | ¥500–1,000/person | 🚶 **Just south of Ueno Park along JR tracks — 5 min walk from museum.** Bustling outdoor market under the train tracks. Street food: *takoyaki*, grilled seafood, fresh fruit. |
| **2:30 PM** | **Ueno Zoo** (optional) | ¥600/adult (~$4), free under 12 (10yo FREE) | 🚶 **Inside Ueno Park, near the museum.** Japan's oldest zoo. Giant pandas, gorillas. Good if kids have energy. Open 9:30–17:00 (Tue–Sun). Skip if museum was enough. |
| **4:00 PM** | Return to Asakusa | — | Walk along the Sumida River for more cherry blossom views. |
| **4:30 PM** | Return to Asakusa, freshen up | — | Quick break at the apartment before the evening event. |
| **5:00 PM** | Head to **Ginza** | ¥170/person | 🚇 **Asakusa Line → Higashi-Ginza (~30 min).** Direct subway — no transfers needed. |
| **5:30 PM** | Arrive **Ginza Inz 1, B1** | — | Venue is directly connected to Ginza Station underground. Look for signage to Ginza Inz 1 basement level. |
| **6:00 PM** | **Tokyo Sumo Festival** ⭐ ✅ BOOKED | ¥48,000–57,600 tickets (~$320–385) + ¥5,000–10,000 food (~$33–67) | 🎭 **The highlight of the trip for both kids!** Watch live sumo matches, learn basic sumo movements, and **actually challenge a real sumo wrestler.** Welcome drink included with ticket. **⚠️ Meals are NOT included** — order food at the venue after arrival (additional charge). Vegetarian/vegan options available. Regular seat: ¥9,600/adult, child (4–12) ¥8,000. 13yo pays adult price, 10yo gets child price. English-speaking staff. ~2.5 hour show. |
| **~8:30 PM** | Train back to Asakusa | ¥170/person | 🚇 **Higashi-Ginza → Asakusa (~30 min).** Same line, reverse direction. |
| **Night** | **Optional Sumida River walk** | Free | If energy permits after the show, walk to Azumabashi Bridge for a stunning nighttime view of Tokyo Skytree lit up across the river. |

**Day 4 spend estimate:** ~$500–610 for the group (Sumo Festival is the big spend of the trip!)

## What I'd SKIP from the ChatGPT List

| Activity | Why Skip |
|----------|----------|
| **Mt. Fuji day trip** | Not feasible. Takes a full day minimum, and you only have 2 days in Tokyo. You'll see Fuji from the Shinkansen and possibly from Tokyo Tower. |
| **Shibuya Sky** | Great observation deck but ¥2,500/person adds up, and the 10yo specifically wants Tokyo Tower. Pick one viewing spot per trip — Tokyo Tower is more iconic and has the history angle. |
| **Kids Plaza Osaka** | Designed for younger kids (ages 3–12). At 13 and 10, your kids are aging out. Skip in favor of Osaka Castle + Den Den Town. |
| **Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan** | Excellent attraction (¥2,700/adult, ¥1,400/child ~7-15) but it's a 3-hour commitment on your only full Osaka day. Save it for a future trip — the castle, food, and cherry blossoms are more unique to this specific timing. |
| **Meiji Shrine** | Beautiful and free, but it's in Harajuku/Shibuya — the opposite direction from your Day 5 route (Akihabara → Tokyo Tower → teamLab). Adding it means backtracking. |
| **Shibuya Crossing / Hachiko** | Iconic but it's literally just crossing a street + a small statue. Not worth the detour when you have limited time. You'll pass through if you happen to transit via Shibuya. |
| **Ueno Zoo** | Optional on Day 4. Only go if the kids want it after the museum. The Tokyo National Museum is a better use of the same time for this group. |
